## Introduction: The Compound Interest of Injustice

The racial wealth gap in America is not a mystery. It is not a cultural phenomenon. It is not explained by individual choices, work ethic, or educational attainment. It is the predictable, mathematically inevitable result of ninety-five years of policy decisions that systematically excluded non-white Americans from wealth-building mechanisms while subsidizing white wealth accumulation with federal dollars.

Here is the core arithmetic: if you pay one group of workers 60 cents for every dollar you pay another group, and you do this for 95 years, and the underpaid group is simultaneously excluded from homeownership (the primary wealth-building vehicle for working Americans), excluded from higher education subsidies, excluded from union membership, excluded from business loans, and disproportionately incarcerated (removing them from the labor force entirely) — the result is not a gap. It is a chasm. And the chasm is not closing. It is widening.

**The Numbers in Brief**  
- Median white household wealth (2022): $285,000 (Federal Reserve SCF)  
- Median Black household wealth (2022): $44,900 (Federal Reserve SCF)  
- Ratio: **6.3 to 1**  
- Median Hispanic household wealth (2022): $61,600 (ratio to white: 4.6 to 1)  
- Women’s earnings (2024): 83.6 cents per dollar (BLS)  
- Black women vs. white men (2024): 69 cents per dollar  
- Hispanic women vs. white men (2024): 57 cents per dollar  

These numbers did not happen by accident. Every one of them traces to identifiable policy decisions made between 1931 and today. This book traces those decisions, documents their effects with federal data, and calculates the compound cost.

The gap is not a mystery. It is arithmetic.
